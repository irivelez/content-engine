### Detailed Day-by-Day Curriculum

#### Day 0: Welcome Email (sent immediately on signup)
**Purpose**: Set expectations, establish authority, deliver first micro-win
**Content**:
- Who is Irina (2 sentences: SF, 10+ yrs enterprise, builds with AI daily)
- What this course IS (practical, builder-focused, Spanish-native)
- What this course IS NOT (theory, motivational, ChatGPT 101)
- The 5-day roadmap (visual preview)
- Micro-task: "Antes de manana, abre Claude.ai y preguntale: 'Cuales son las 3 decisiones mas importantes que debo tomar esta semana en mi trabajo?' — lee la respuesta. Manana te enseno por que esa respuesta fue mediocre y como mejorarla 10x."
- P.D.: "Responde este email con tu rol y que esperas lograr. Leo cada respuesta." (segmentation + engagement signal)

#### Day 1: Professional Prompting (The Foundation)
**Theme**: "El error que el 90% comete con AI"
**Core lesson**: Most people prompt AI like Google (question → answer). Professional prompting is directive: role + context + constraints + output format.
**Irina's example**: Show a real before/after prompt from her daily work (e.g., research prompt for content, analysis prompt for decisions)
**Framework**: The RCCO method — Rol, Contexto, Constraints, Output
**Exercise**: Take yesterday's Claude response. Rewrite the prompt using RCCO. Compare results.
**Tool focus**: Claude (primary) + ChatGPT (comparison)
**Bridge**: "Manana: un prompt perfecto no sirve de nada si lo usas en aislamiento. Te enseno como conectar AI a tu flujo de trabajo real."

#### Day 2: Your First System (Workflows)
**Theme**: "Deja de copiar y pegar entre herramientas"
**Core lesson**: Individual prompts = linear. Systems = exponential. A workflow connects AI to your actual tools (email, docs, calendar, data).
**Irina's example**: Her daily content research workflow (fetch → AI curation → output). Show the before (30 min manual) vs after (5 min automated).
**Framework**: The INPUT → PROCESS → OUTPUT model for any AI workflow
**Exercise**: Map one repetitive task from their work. Identify the INPUT (what triggers it), PROCESS (what AI does), OUTPUT (where the result goes). Write the workflow on paper.
**Tool mention**: n8n, Make, Zapier (show awareness of options, don't force one tool)
**Bridge**: "Ya sabes como hacer que AI trabaje EN tu flujo. Manana: como hacer que AI te ayude a PENSAR, no solo a producir."

#### Day 3: AI for Decisions (Strategic Use)
**Theme**: "AI no es solo para producir"
**Core lesson**: Most people use AI for output (write this, summarize that). The real leverage is using AI for analysis, evaluation, and decision-making.
**Irina's example**: How she uses Claude to evaluate business decisions (pros/cons analysis, market research, risk assessment)
**Framework**: The Decision Amplifier — feed AI your context + options + constraints → get structured analysis
**Exercise**: Pick a real decision they're facing at work. Build a "decision prompt" with: Context, Options, Constraints, "Analyze the tradeoffs and recommend an approach with reasoning."
**Target audience resonance**: ICP-1 (Camila) will light up — this is what she needs for board meetings
**Bridge**: "Ahora sabes dirigir AI para pensar. Manana: construimos algo tangible que puedes mostrar a tu equipo."

#### Day 4: Build Something Real (30 Minutes)
**Theme**: "Construye algo real en 30 minutos"
**Core lesson**: The gap between "user" and "builder" is smaller than you think. With Claude Artifacts or ChatGPT Canvas, you can build functional tools without writing code.
**Irina's example**: "I built an internal dashboard/calculator/decision tool in 20 minutes. Here's exactly how."
**Framework**: The Build Sprint — Define (what problem?), Prompt (describe the solution), Iterate (refine 2-3 times), Ship (use it or share it)
**Exercise**: Build a simple tool for their work. Options:
  - A calculator/estimator for something they do weekly
  - A dashboard that organizes data they look at regularly
  - A template generator for repetitive documents
**Tool focus**: Claude Artifacts (preferred) or ChatGPT Canvas
**Bridge**: "Construiste algo real. Eso te separa del 95% de profesionales que 'planean usar AI'. Manana: como mantener este momentum y convertirlo en ventaja permanente."

#### Day 5: From User to Implementer (Identity Shift)
**Theme**: "Lo que separa a los que implementan de los que planifican"
**Core lesson**: AI adoption isn't a project — it's an identity. The people who win aren't the ones who "plan to implement AI"; they're the ones who already are. Today we build your personal 90-day roadmap.
**Irina's example**: Her own journey — from first prompt to building multi-agent systems. Not presented as "beginner story" but as "here's the implementation roadmap I followed."
**Framework**: The 90-Day AI Roadmap
  - Days 1-30: Master one tool (deep, not wide)
  - Days 31-60: Build your first system (connect tools to workflow)
  - Days 61-90: Share what you've built (visibility = opportunities)
**Exercise**: Fill in their personalized 90-day roadmap (template provided)
**Transition to paid**: "Si quieres implementar esto con un grupo de lideres LATAM y mi guia directa, estoy preparando algo. Responde 'ME INTERESA' y te aviso primero." (soft waitlist CTA)

### Post-Course Transition

After Day 5, subscriber enters the regular newsletter ("AI con Irina" or the branded name). The welcome sequence (5-7 emails over 2 weeks) bridges from course to ongoing relationship:

1. Day 7: "Que construiste?" — follow-up on Day 4/5 exercises, community building
2. Day 10: Irina's story (credibility: SF, enterprise, hackathon, YC)
3. Day 14: Best LinkedIn post ever (social proof, invite to follow)
4. Day 17: "El problema que nadie habla" (pain point + framework)
5. Day 21: Soft pitch for paid cohort (waitlist or early access)

## 6. RESEARCH-BACKED BENCHMARKS (April 2026)

### Email Course Performance Data
- Well-structured 5-day email courses achieve **40-60% completion** (vs 5-13% for standard online courses)
- People who complete an exercise on Day 1 are **3-4x more likely to finish** the full course
- Welcome email has **60-80% open rate** — the highest of any email you'll ever send to that subscriber
- Day 3 is the critical retention point — those who survive Day 3 tend to finish
- **300-500 words per lesson** is the sweet spot for educational emails (longer than marketing emails)

### Subject Line Data
- **2-4 word subject lines**: 46% open rate (highest performing length)
- **Lowercase** outperforms title case
- **Personalized subject lines**: 31% higher open rate
- **Pattern variation** across the 5 days prevents fatigue (curiosity → question → contrarian → action → urgency)

### Landing Page Conversion Data
- Average lead magnet landing page: 18% conversion
- Well-optimized with tight value proposition: **25-30%+**
- Email-only form (no name field) converts highest — each extra field = -11% conversion
- Benefit-specific headline beats clever/vague by 2-3x
- No navigation links (every exit link is a leak)
- Mobile-first: 70%+ of LinkedIn traffic is mobile

### Free-to-Paid Conversion Data
- Email course completers → paid product: **5-10% conversion** (vs 1.5-2% for general email list)
- Optimal pitch window: Days 6-8 after course ends (3 emails max)
- Pitching BEFORE Day 5 completion **destroys trust** — deliver 100% of promised value first
- Day 5 should create an "aspirational gap" that the paid product fills

### Beehiiv Technical Notes
- Automations require **Scale plan** (not available on free/Growth)
- Supports: drag-and-drop workflow, conditional branching, tagging, chaining automations
- Use separate signup forms per traffic source (LinkedIn post, bio link, etc.) for attribution
- Set trigger: "subscriber joins via [specific form]" → 24-hour delays between lessons
- Tag subscribers: "sprint-ai-enrolled" on signup, "sprint-ai-completed" after Day 5
- Conditional branch after Day 5: engaged completers → pitch sequence; disengaged → re-engagement
