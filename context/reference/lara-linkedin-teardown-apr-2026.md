> **Purpose:** Distill what actually drives her engagement in April 2026 and convert it into concrete changes to this pipeline. Keep it simple. Adopt immediately.

## TL;DR — The 5 rules that explain her feed

1. **~44% of her posts are NOT teaching. They are authority signals.** (Forbes, speaking stages, HQ visits, reposts of others praising her.) Content is half "look what I know", half "look where I am".
2. **Media first, text second.** Of 16 original posts: 11 multi-image, 3 video, 0 text-only. Video gets the highest average engagement (~2,560 reactions). Pure text died.
3. **Every viral hook has a number in it or a vulnerability in it.** 375 days. 80+ people. 90 days. 42hrs. $100K. Or: "my parents came from nothing", "I could downplay it", "I was told I'd never make it."
4. **CTAs are stacked, not single.** The typical post ends with 2–3 CTAs: repost-gated resource + comment-bait question + direct link. Rarely just one.
5. **She writes in Builder Mode 100% of the time.** Every post ties to a specific thing she built, shipped, won, or tried. Zero philosopher mode. Zero "reflexión de media noche".

## Pattern 1 — Hooks (what's actually working)

Every hook in the top 10 posts falls into one of five buckets. These are the only hook types you need.

### A. Personal vulnerability (2-3 lines)
> "my parents came from nothing and still gave me everything." (3,725 reactions)
> "I could downplay getting into Forbes 30u30 last week. / Truth is, I've wanted this more than anything." (1,946)
> "With love, a girl who was told she'd never make it." (closing line)

**Why it works:** Lowercase, short, emotional paradox. No jargon. Sets up the whole post as memoir, not lecture.

### B. Milestone all-caps (rare, reserve for big wins)
> "I JUST MADE IT TO FORBES 30 UNDER 30!" (3,692)

**Rule:** One per quarter, max. Devalues fast.

### C. Numeric specificity
> "I spent the last 90 days testing every growth hack I could find." (2,353)
> "Over the weekend, in front of 80+ people, I shared my most important piece of advice." (1,776)
> "Leveraged LinkedIn and AI to make $100,000 fast." (body, post 9)

**Rule:** A number in line 1 or line 2 of every tactical post. Must be specific, not round.

### D. Contrarian negation
> "You don't need algorithm hacks to grow on LinkedIn." (2,353)
> "You don't need an office. You don't need a bloated team. You don't need to raise investment." (post 9)
> "You need to stop being humble." (1,776)

**Rule:** Start from the assumption you're breaking. "Everyone says X. But…"

### E. Reassurance (for tactical video)
> "If you're feeling overwhelmed right now, listen to this. / You're not bad at things. You're just new at them." (1,936)

**Rule:** Hook as emotional reassurance → framework in body. Works beautifully for selfie-talking-head video.

**Anti-patterns in this sample:** Zero philosopher hooks. Zero "Reflexión de...". Zero quotes-by-famous-people openers. Zero rhetorical questions as first line (she uses rhetorical questions inside the body, never as the hook).

## Pattern 2 — Body structure (3 shapes)

### Shape 1: Anaphora triad
Repeat the same clause start 3x.
- "Stop being humble about your achievements. / Stop being humble about your business. / Stop being humble about your offer."
- "You don't need an office. / You don't need a bloated team. / You don't need to raise investment."

### Shape 2: Numbered framework (3–4 steps, never more)
- Post 6: 3 steps (Hyper-specific content / Proof-first / LinkedIn Video)
- Post 10: 3 takeaways
- Post 14: 4 steps (find → execute → validate → double down)

Never 7 steps. Never 10. She keeps it to 3 or 4.

### Shape 3: Before/After arc
- Post 1: 375 days of work → Forbes moment → parents' reaction
- Post 5: Vision board at 23 → "too late" doubts → Forbes list
- Post 8: Door-to-door student days in Birmingham → keynote speaker today

## Pattern 3 — CTAs (always stacked)

The typical post has **2 or 3 CTAs**, not one. In order of frequency:

1. **Comment-bait PS question** — 7 of 16 posts
   - "PS: What is that 1 big goal you'd like to celebrate with your parents this year?"
   - "PS: Which step do you struggle with the most?"
   - "PS: What is 1 thing you'd ask LinkedIn if you went to their HQ? Comment below!"

2. **Repost-gated resource** — 4 posts, all high-repost
   - "If you want it ♻️ repost this post and I'll send it to you (20 people max)." → 122 reposts
   - "Repost this if you're on the way to building the life of your dreams too ♻️" → 78 reposts
   - Value exchange: you repost, she DMs you a video/PDF/tool.

3. **Direct link to masterclass / lead magnet / tool** — 6 posts
   - Usually at the end of the PS or as a third line after repost CTA.

4. **Scarcity wrapper** — 2 posts but huge effect
   - "for the next 42hrs" (Post 18)
   - "20 people max" (Post 6)

5. **Mystery teaser** — 2 posts
   - "I'm bringing on a special guest, any guesses?"
   - "Can you spot a familiar face in this photo?"

**Rule for us:** Every tactical post should close with **at least 2 CTAs**: one comment-bait, one link or repost. Pure authority/milestone posts can skip the link, but still need a comment prompt.

## Pattern 4 — Visual packaging (what she actually posts)

### Multi-image carousel (dominant format)
- 11 of 16 posts. Usually 3–5 images.
- Hero image is either:
  - **Event photo** (speaking stage, conference, travel)
  - **Saveable infographic** (Post 6: "4-quadrant LinkedIn algorithm design")
  - **Forbes cover / Kleo screenshot** (authority proof)
- Subsequent images are proof layers: handwritten goals, screenshots, team photos.

### Video (highest engagement)
- All videos are **selfie-style, talking to camera**
- Caption overlay on key phrase ("is ignore ideas" on the overwhelmed video)
- Lowercase, short captions under the video
- Raw feel. No corporate polish.

### What's missing from her feed
- No text-only posts in this 18-post sample
- No AI-generated slick graphics
- No stock photos
- No quote cards over nature backgrounds

## Pattern 5 — Halo-effect mechanics (the cheat code)

Three of her 16 posts in this sample are **third-party reposts of other creators praising her** — and they got 363, 475, and 2,337 reactions. Zero writing effort.

How she does it:
- Attends an event / coaches / collaborates → other creators post about her → she reposts their post.
- The Jasmin Alić post ("I invited Lara to Bosnia to coach 50+ creators") got 2,337 reactions on her feed — more than most of her own tactical posts.

**Why this matters for us:** We don't need to write every viral post. Collaboration → others publish → we repost = free authority.

---

## What NOT to copy

1. **Don't chase her volume of authority flexes.** She has Forbes, a $100K case, Kleo funded, 325K followers. Her posts work because of cumulative proof. Early-stage creators flexing similar hooks sound hollow.
2. **Don't lowercase everything.** Her lowercase-style works in context of her brand. In Spanish LATAM B2B context it can read as careless.
3. **Don't copy "I made $100K fast" hooks.** Dollar proof without a case study behind it is empty calories.
4. **Don't over-stack CTAs on every post.** Her repost-gated mechanic works because she has a big list. For us, a single clean CTA often lands better — stack only on tactical "big" posts.
5. **Don't write 20-line anaphora posts.** She uses 3 items. We'd see diminishing returns past 3.
