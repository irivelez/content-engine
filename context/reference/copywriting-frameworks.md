# Copywriting Frameworks Reference

> This document provides detailed breakdowns of proven copywriting frameworks that can be applied to LinkedIn content.

## Overview
Copywriting frameworks are tested formulas that guide content structure to maximize persuasion, clarity, and engagement. These frameworks come from decades of marketing and sales experience and can be adapted for social media content.

---

## Core Frameworks

### 1. AIDA (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action)
**Origin**: Classic advertising formula from 1898
**Best For**: Sales-oriented content, product launches, promotional posts

**Structure**:
```
A - Attention: Grab attention with compelling hook
I - Interest: Build interest with relevant information
D - Desire: Create desire by showing benefits/transformation
A - Action: Clear call-to-action
```

**LinkedIn Application**:
```
Attention: "I just hit $10K MRR with a product I built in 2 weeks"

Interest: "No fancy tech stack. No big team. Just a simple tool that solves one problem really well."

Desire: "If I can do this while working full-time, anyone can. Here's the exact process:"

Action: "Want my step-by-step guide? Drop a comment and I'll DM you."
```

**When to Use**:
- Launching a product or service
- Promoting a resource or offer
- Teaching a process with clear outcome

---

### 2. PAS (Problem, Agitate, Solve)
**Origin**: Classic sales copywriting
**Best For**: Addressing pain points, offering solutions, positioning expertise

**Structure**:
```
P - Problem: Identify the challenge your audience faces
A - Agitate: Amplify the pain/frustration
S - Solve: Present your solution or perspective
```

**LinkedIn Application**:
```
Problem: "You spend hours on LinkedIn posts that get 10 likes."

Agitate: "Meanwhile, someone else posts 5 lines and gets 1,000+ engagements. You wonder: 'What am I doing wrong?' The frustration builds. You consider giving up."

Solve: "The difference isn't luck. It's understanding the psychology of attention. Here are the 3 principles every viral post uses:"
```

**When to Use**:
- Your audience has a clear pain point
- You're offering a solution (advice, product, service)
- You want to position as problem-solver

**Caution**: Don't over-agitate - can feel manipulative if too heavy-handed

---

### 3. BAB (Before, After, Bridge)
**Origin**: Transformation marketing
**Best For**: Personal stories, case studies, teaching methodology

**Structure**:
```
Before: Describe the struggle/starting state
After: Show the transformation/end state
Bridge: Explain how to get from before to after
```

**LinkedIn Application**:
```
Before: "A year ago I was working 60-hour weeks and barely making ends meet."

After: "Today I work 25 hours a week and make 3x my old salary."

Bridge: "The shift happened when I stopped trading time for money and started building systems. Here's the 3-step process I used:"
```

**When to Use**:
- Sharing personal transformation
- Teaching a process or system
- Demonstrating proof of concept

---

### 4. FAB (Features, Advantages, Benefits)
**Origin**: Sales training methodology
**Best For**: Explaining value, product/service descriptions, educational content

**Structure**:
```
F - Features: What it is/does (facts)
A - Advantages: Why that matters (logic)
B - Benefits: What's in it for them (emotion)
```

**LinkedIn Application**:
```
Feature: "I created a content calendar system in Notion."

Advantage: "It auto-generates post ideas based on trending topics and my past high-performers."

Benefit: "Now I spend 30 minutes planning instead of 3 hours staring at a blank screen. More time creating, less time overthinking."
```

**When to Use**:
- Sharing tools or systems
- Explaining why something matters
- Teaching value-focused thinking

---

### 5. The 4 Ps (Picture, Promise, Prove, Push)
**Origin**: Modern content marketing
**Best For**: Visionary content, big ideas, thought leadership

**Structure**:
```
Picture: Paint a vision of what's possible
Promise: State what you'll deliver
Prove: Provide evidence or credibility
Push: Call to action
```

**LinkedIn Application**:
```
Picture: "Imagine creating a month of content in 2 hours. No stress. No writer's block. Just consistent, quality posts."

Promise: "I'm going to show you the exact system I use to batch-create 30 LinkedIn posts."

Prove: "This system took me from posting 2x/week (inconsistently) to daily posting for 6 months straight. My engagement tripled."

Push: "Bookmark this. You'll want to reference it when you build your own system."
```

**When to Use**:
- Sharing big ideas or visions
- Teaching comprehensive systems
- Establishing thought leadership

---

### 6. PASTOR (Problem, Amplify, Story, Transformation, Offer, Response)
**Origin**: Ray Edwards copywriting framework
**Best For**: Long-form content, storytelling posts, high-value offers

**Structure**:
```
P - Problem: Identify the pain
A - Amplify: Deepen understanding of the problem
S - Story: Share relevant story/example
T - Transformation: Show the change that's possible
O - Offer: Present your solution
R - Response: Clear next step
```

**LinkedIn Application**:
```
Problem: "Most creators burn out in 3 months."

Amplify: "They start with huge energy. Post daily. Engage constantly. Then life gets busy. They miss a day. Then a week. Then they quit. The pattern is predictable."

Story: "I did this twice before I figured it out. My third attempt? I'm 18 months in and just getting started."

Transformation: "The difference was building systems instead of relying on motivation."

Offer: "I documented the entire system - content calendar, batch creation process, engagement workflow."

Response: "Comment 'SYSTEM' if you want it. I'll DM you the template."
```

**When to Use**:
- Long-form storytelling
- High-value resource offers
- Building deep engagement

---

### 7. The 4 Us (Useful, Urgent, Unique, Ultra-specific)
**Origin**: Direct response marketing
**Best For**: Headlines, hooks, value-focused content

**Structure**:
```
Useful: Provides clear value
Urgent: Creates timeliness/importance
Unique: Offers distinct perspective
Ultra-specific: Concrete and detailed
```

**LinkedIn Application**:
```
Useful: "How to grow LinkedIn followers"
+ Urgent: "...in Q1 2025 (before the algorithm changes)"
+ Unique: "...without posting daily"
+ Ultra-specific: "...from 500 to 5,000 in 90 days"

Result: "How to grow from 500 to 5,000 LinkedIn followers in 90 days without posting daily (before the Q1 2025 algorithm changes)"
```

**When to Use**:
- Crafting headlines and hooks
- Positioning offers or resources
- Standing out in crowded spaces

## Content Structure Frameworks

### The Inverted Pyramid
**Origin**: Journalism
**Best For**: News-style posts, announcements, quick insights

**Structure**:
```
1. Most important info first (headline/hook)
2. Supporting details
3. Background context
4. Additional resources
```

**When to Use**: When main point should be immediately clear

---

### The Hero's Journey (Simplified)
**Origin**: Joseph Campbell storytelling framework
**Best For**: Personal narratives, transformation stories

**Structure**:
```
1. Ordinary World (before state)
2. Call to Adventure (catalyst for change)
3. Challenges (obstacles faced)
4. Transformation (what changed)
5. Return with Gift (lesson/value for others)
```

**LinkedIn Application**:
```
Ordinary: "I was a corporate employee dreaming of freedom."
Call: "My company announced layoffs. I had 90 days."
Challenges: "$47 in savings. No freelance experience. Imposter syndrome."
Transformation: "I learned to sell my skills instead of waiting for permission."
Gift: "Here are the 5 lessons that changed everything for me..."
```

---

### The Socratic Method
**Origin**: Ancient Greek philosophy
**Best For**: Challenging assumptions, thought leadership

**Structure**:
```
1. Ask a question
2. Examine the common answer
3. Challenge with counter-questions
4. Arrive at deeper truth
```

**LinkedIn Application**:
```
Question: "Should you post daily on LinkedIn?"

Common answer: "Yes, the algorithm rewards consistency."

Challenge: "But what if your daily posts are mediocre? What if posting 3x/week with great content outperforms 7x/week with okay content?"

Truth: "Consistency matters. But quality consistency beats quantity always."
```

## Engagement-Specific Frameworks

### The Pattern Interrupt
**Structure**:
```
1. Say something unexpected
2. Explain why it's actually true
3. Provide the reframe
4. Invite perspective sharing
```

**Example**:
```
"Stop trying to go viral.

Seriously.

Chasing virality is like chasing happiness - the more you chase it, the further it runs.

Instead, focus on creating 'viral-worthy' content consistently. One will hit. You just don't know which one.

What's your take on viral content?"
```

---

### The Listicle Framework
**Structure**:
```
1. Numbered promise in hook
2. Brief context (why this matters)
3. Each item with explanation
4. Summary or next step
```

**Best for**: Actionable tips, curated insights, frameworks

## Framework Selection Guide

### Choose Based on Goal:

**Selling/Promoting**: AIDA, FAB, PASTOR
**Problem-Solving**: PAS, BAB
**Storytelling**: Hero's Journey, BAB, PASTOR
**Teaching**: 4 Ps, Listicle, Inverted Pyramid
**Thought Leadership**: Socratic Method, Pattern Interrupt
**Quick Value**: 4 Us, Inverted Pyramid

### Choose Based on Content Type:

**Personal Story**: BAB, Hero's Journey
**Educational**: FAB, 4 Ps, Listicle
**Contrarian Take**: Socratic Method, Pattern Interrupt, PAS
**Resource Offer**: AIDA, PASTOR
**Industry Commentary**: Inverted Pyramid, Socratic Method

## Combining Frameworks

You can layer frameworks for maximum impact:

**Example**: AIDA (overall structure) + PAS (problem section) + 4 Us (hook)

```
Hook (4 Us): "How I 10x'd my engagement in 30 days without spending a dollar (ultra-specific, urgent, unique, useful)"

Attention (AIDA) + Problem (PAS): "Your LinkedIn posts are getting ignored."

Interest (AIDA) + Agitate (PAS): "You spend hours writing. Get 12 likes. Wonder if it's worth it."

Desire (AIDA) + Solve (PAS): "I changed 3 things. Everything shifted. Here's what worked:"

Action (AIDA): "Save this post. You'll need these principles for your next post."
```

## Framework Application Checklist

Before using a framework, ask:

- ✅ Does this framework fit my goal? (persuade, educate, inspire, etc.)
- ✅ Does it match my voice? (some frameworks feel more formal/casual)
- ✅ Is it appropriate for this topic?
- ✅ Will my audience respond to this structure?
- ✅ Can I execute it authentically?

**Remember**: Frameworks are scaffolding. Your voice and value are the building.

---

## Instructions for AI Agents

When using frameworks:

1. **Select appropriately**: Match framework to content goal
2. **Adapt, don't rigidly apply**: Frameworks are guides, not rules
3. **Maintain voice**: Author's style trumps framework purity
4. **Explain your choice**: Tell the author why you chose this framework
5. **Be flexible**: Mix frameworks if it serves the content better

The best content uses framework structure with authentic execution.
