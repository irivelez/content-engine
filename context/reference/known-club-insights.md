## OPERATE NOW: Actions & Frameworks

### Action 1: Define Your "Friend Test" Statement
**Framework (Tasleem):** "I help **[who]** do **[what]** so they can **[result]**."
- Must pass the test: could a friend repeat this to someone at a dinner party?
- This statement drives your headline, bio, content pillars, and offer positioning.
- **Status: UNRESOLVED** — needs a working session to define.

### Action 2: Pick Your Polarizing Enemy
**Framework (Tasleem):** Choose ONE thing you stand against. This differentiates your brand and attracts people who share your view.
- Examples from course: "sloppy sales," "work-life balance," "success-induced chaos"
- Candidates for Irina: generic AI hype? Fear-mongering about AI replacing jobs? Corporate inertia? Waiting to adopt AI? "AI gurus" who don't build?
- **Status: UNRESOLVED** — needs decision.

### Action 3: Design Your First Offer (The Offer Ladder)
**Framework (Shan + Lara):**

| Tier | Price | Purpose | Priority |
|------|-------|---------|----------|
| Lead Magnet | Free | Email capture | DO FIRST |
| High-Ticket | $5K-$10K+ | Core revenue (consulting, cohort, done-with-you) | DO SECOND |
| Downsell | $997-$3K | DIY version for price objections | LATER |
| Low-Ticket | $99 | Lead acquisition / cover ad spend | SKIP FOR NOW |

**Key rules:**
- Sell before you build. Validate by selling the outcome first.
- Avoid $99 products without massive audience. Focus high-ticket first.
- Free consults = sales calls for high-ticket. Downsell to DIY if price is the objection.
- Reverse-engineer the market: analyze competitors' products, pricing, reviews. Sell what's already selling.
- **Status: UNRESOLVED** — needs offer design session.

### Action 4: Start the 2-Month Content Warm-Up
**Framework (Lara) — 4 posts/week:**
1. **Problem-Solving**: How-to guides, playbooks. Hook with highest-leverage social proof.
2. **Storytelling**: Personal narratives showing transformation. "I did X and now I'm Y."
3. **Authority**: Event photos, results screenshots, customer success. Visuals are the "bait."
4. **Lead Magnet**: Free resource to build email list. Visual must be compelling (GIF > static image).

**Already operational:** LinkedIn content engine handles pillars 1-3. Missing: lead magnet creation + email list building.
- **Status: PARTIALLY OPERATIONAL** — need lead magnet + email nurture sequence.

### Action 5: Apply the 4-3-2-1 Content Mix
**Framework (Lara):**
- 4 broad posts (AI changing the world — reach)
- 3 niche posts (specific AI implementation — authority)
- 2 proof posts (results, screenshots — trust)
- 1 story post (personal journey, contrarian views — connection)

**Can integrate immediately** into content-engine weekly planning.

### Action 6: Upgrade Hooks with CTE
**Framework (Lara):** Use Call-to-Engage over Call-to-Action for authority posts.
- CTE example: "Out of these 5 steps, which is the hardest for your team?"
- Reserve direct CTAs for lead magnet posts and launch content only.
- **Can apply immediately** to every post.

### Action 7: Build Content Series
**Framework (Tasleem):** Repeatable formats build audience expectation and brand association.
- Example: weekly "AI tool I tested this week" or "What I built with AI this week"
- Creates habit loops in the audience.
- **Can apply immediately** — choose one series format and commit.

### Action 8: Quick Cash from Warm Leads (When Revenue Is Urgent)
**Framework (Tasleem):** Time-sensitive, tailored upsell to warm leads (engaged followers, email list, past prospects). Fastest path to revenue.
- **Available when needed** — requires defined offer first (Action 3).

## AUTHORITY POSITIONING (Deep Reference)

### The Authority Flywheel (Lara)
- "How I" > "How to" — Personal stories build more trust than generic advice
- Packaging is the primary hook — Screenshots, event photos, visuals turn 6/10 posts into 10/10
- Storytelling is the one thing AI cannot replicate — Use unique stories of trials and revelations
- Show proof upfront — For outreach, provide immediate value (Loom video, drafted posts) before asking for a call
- Gradual transitions — Frame pivots as evolution based on new insights, not failure

### The 3 E's of a Memorable Brand (Tasleem)
1. **Easy to Remember** — Simple associations reduce cognitive load. One symbol, one tagline, one emotion.
2. **Easy to Describe** — Clear job in a clear context. Uber = "Get a ride, from your phone, on demand."
3. **Easy to Refer** — Clear villain + simple action. Calendly = villain (scheduling back-and-forth), action ("send your Calendly link").

### Brain Rules for Content (Tasleem)
- **Brain is lazy** — Be simple. Clear CTAs. Rhyme in endings for memorability.
- **Brain loves patterns** — Break them. Subvert common post formats = pattern interrupt.
- **Brain cares about relevancy** — Hooks signal who content is FOR. Broad hooks = reach. Narrow = conversion.

### Brain Levers for Standing Out (Tasleem)
1. **Clarity** — The "friend test" statement
2. **Differentiation** — Polarizing enemy
3. **Repetition** — Themes, visuals, frameworks across posts
4. **Personality Wavelength** — Vary emotional tone. Always-positive = pattern the brain ignores.

### Bonus Tactics (Tasleem)
- Build parasocial relationships with personal language
- Signal success subtly through stories that hint at authority
- Develop insider language — coin terms for your community
- Do unscalable things — one memorable, one-off act for massive brand recall

### Positioning for Pivots (Lara)
- **Venn Diagram Method**: Old role + new role = overlap is your unique advantage
- Content strategy for pivots: use "How I" stories to bridge old and new identity
- Frame changes as evolution, not failure

## MONETIZATION & OFFER CREATION (Deep Reference)

### The $100K Launch Blueprint (Lara) — 3 Phases

**Phase 1: Content Warm-Up (2 months pre-launch)**
- 4 posts/week: problem-solving, storytelling, authority, lead magnet
- Build email list via lead magnets with compelling visuals
- Follow-up email sequence to warm the list

**Phase 2: Irresistible Offer Design**
- Timed offer: Limited spots/deadline = scarcity
- Transformation offer: "Get [result] in [timeframe]"
- Bonuses: Address every objection (time? templates. Support? 1-on-1 call)
- Logical offer formula: "How to do X in Y timeframe to get Z result"

**Phase 3: Launch Blitz (4-10 days)**
- Daily content (every post has CTA)
- LinkedIn Lives / webinars (20 min education, 20 min demo, 20 min Q&A + offer)
- Emails: 2-5/day using SLAY framework (Story, Lesson, Action, You)
- Comments section = testimonials, links, proof
- Launch one offer at a time. New offer every 4-6 weeks.
- Use ascension model: launch high-ticket first, downsell if price is objection.

### The Dual-Funnel (Shan)
- **Qualified leads** -> High-ticket services (consulting, done-for-you)
- **Unqualified leads** -> DIY digital products (courses, templates)
- Your brand naturally attracts 90% unqualified leads — monetize them, don't ignore them

### Monetizing Digital Products (Shan)
1. **Map your skills** — All hard + soft skills. Your "common knowledge" is often highly valuable.
2. **Understand audience needs** — Surveys, interviews, sentiment analysis of competitor comments.
3. **Reverse-engineer the market** — Competitor products, pricing, reviews. Tools: SimilarWeb, Meta Pixel Helper.
4. **Structure the product** — "Zero-to-one" journey. 5-8 modules, 4-8 videos each. Module 1 = your story + credibility. Be prescriptive ("You must do X"), not vague.
5. **Sell before you build** — Validate with pre-sales.

### Outreach That Converts (Shan)
1. Warm up (2 weeks): Engage with prospect's content
2. Problem-led approach: Research their business, find specific problem
3. Give the answer: Detailed solution in first contact
4. Open-loop CTA: "What do you think?" not "book a call"
5. Follow-ups: Fresh angle each time (new value), not reminders

### "Trojan Horse" Outreach (Lara)
1. Value first: Custom Loom video or drafted posts for prospect
2. Low-pressure CTA: Offer a call to discuss
3. Close: Convert on the call

### Pricing Framework (Shan)
- See: `context/reference/High Converting Offers (TKC).md`
- Base Only (simple) vs Base + Performance (partner/premium)
- Custom offers only — no fixed packages
- Always maintain profitability including downsell/upsell options

### Business Model Principles (Lara)
- Prioritize cash flow over branding — launch simple paid service first
- Stop free work — attracts clients who don't value your services
- Avoid low-ticket products at early stage — $99 is unprofitable without massive audience
- Leverage multiple ICPs with consistent core message — one weekly story post for secondary ICP

### Scaling (Shan)
- Move down the funnel: awareness -> conversion/retention = higher revenue
- Base + Performance pricing aligns incentives
- Evolve services as client needs arise (prevents them outgrowing you)
- Pay clients for referrals (lifetime % of revenue)
- Attention is the hardest asset to acquire — correlates directly with income
