## The "Serves Both" Principle

The best content for your business serves ALL three tiers simultaneously. You don't need separate content tracks — you need one content strategy with different conversion paths.

| Content Type | Fan Base Gets | ICP Gets |
|-------------|---------------|----------|
| **Builder demo** ("Built X in 30 min") | "I can do this too!" | "My team needs to learn this" |
| **Resource curation** ("4 companies dropped AI playbooks") | Free resources to try | Strategic intel for decisions |
| **Resistance story** ("I resisted n8n until...") | Permission to start | Proof that adoption is worth it |
| **AI news analysis** ("What GPT-5 means for you") | Understanding | Strategic planning input |
| **Step-by-step guide** | Skill they apply | Training material for their team |

**Key insight**: Builder Mode content naturally bridges all tiers. A post about building something in 30 minutes inspires the fan base AND gives ICP-1 a business case for AI adoption AND gives ICP-2 a playbook to replicate.

## Content Preferences

### Format
- Short paragraphs (2-3 lines max), generous white space, mobile-scannable
- Step-by-step guides for LinkedIn (3-5 min read)
- Demo screenshots/videos for builder posts
- Canva visuals for resource curation
- Deep dives reserved for newsletter (Guia Practica)

### Depth
- LinkedIn: Quick tips (1-2 min) to medium (3-5 min)
- Newsletter: Deep practical guides (1000-1500 words)
- Always skimmable — they scan on mobile

### Technical Level
- Semi-technical: concepts with clear analogies, no code or math
- English tech terms fine when natural (AI, prompts, Claude Code, n8n)
- "Show, don't tell" — demo beats explanation every time

### Tone
- Conversational but credible (primary)
- Tu form always (never usted)
- Honest, energetic, confident builder
- NOT: corporate buzzwords, guru energy, generic LinkedIn-speak, hype without substance

## Content to Avoid (Performance-Validated)

- **English posts** — Catastrophic performance. No exceptions.
- **Philosopher mode** — Abstract concepts without building/doing anchor. Fails every time.
- **Borrowed authority** — Steve Jobs/Elon quotes without personal experience.
- **Encyclopedia lists** — "7 AI Concepts" without "I used this when..." grounding.
- **Generic motivation** — Sunset photos + life advice = could be anyone.
- **"Reflexion de media noche"** — Signals random thought, not structured insight.
- **Multi-agent architecture diagrams** — Too technical even for ICP-1.
- **Promo-first posts** — Leading with selling instead of value.

## AI Knowledge Level (Early 2026)

### Current State
- Most have used ChatGPT; many experimenting with Claude and Gemini
- Aware AI is reshaping their industry but unclear on HOW to apply it
- Can follow AI news but struggle to move from "interesting" to "implemented"
- Growing awareness of AI agents and automation (seen as "advanced" / not for them yet)

### The Critical Gap
**From experimenting to integrating**: They've tried ChatGPT for fun prompts. They haven't figured out how to make AI part of their actual daily workflow. This is the gap your content bridges.

### Tool Familiarity
- **Common**: ChatGPT (universal), some Gemini and Claude

- **Emerging**: n8n, Make, Zapier AI, Claude Projects, Custom GPTs
- **Not on radar**: LangChain, LlamaIndex, vector databases, custom dev

## ICP Income Context: LATAM vs. US

LATAM wages are roughly **30-35% lower than US** for equivalent positions. When we say "$5,000+ USD/month" for ICP-1, this means:

| Role (LATAM) | Typical Monthly (USD) | US Equivalent |
|-------------|----------------------|---------------|
| Senior Manager / Director | $4,000-8,000 | $6,000-12,000 |
| CTO / CIO / VP | $6,000-15,000 | $10,000-25,000 |
| Founder (profitable) | $8,000-25,000+ | $15,000-40,000+ |

**Key insight**: These people have real purchasing power in LATAM. A $2,000 workshop or $500/month advisory retainer is within their budget — especially if framed as business investment, not personal expense (corporate training budget, not personal wallet).

**The funnel**: Free content (LinkedIn) → Newsletter (email) → Low-ticket (fan base) → High-ticket (ICP)
