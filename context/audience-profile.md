# Audience Profile

**Last Updated**: 2026-03-31
**Version**: 4.0
**Data Source**: LinkedIn Analytics (March 5 - April 1, 2026)
**Purpose**: Guide AI agents in content creation, angle selection, tone calibration, and branding decisions. This document describes the audience — who they are, what they know, what they need, and how they consume.

---

## 1. Audience Identity

These are game changers. Decision makers. Disruptors — or becoming one. They are deeply curious, constantly growing, and driven by something bigger than their current title. Some are vocal; many are quietly building in the background. They may be cautious, but they have drive. They don't want to be told what to think — they want to see what's coming so they can act first.

They work across LATAM in technology, consulting, telecom, and financial services. They range from C-suite executives running enterprise AI strategy to ambitious professionals learning to build with AI for the first time. What unites them: they know AI is reshaping everything, and they refuse to be passive about it.

### Three Archetypes

**Camila — The Corporate Strategist (ICP-1)**
VP of Digital Transformation at a 2,000-person Colombian telecom. Reports to the CEO. Has budget authority but needs ammunition — proof, case studies, ROI framing — to justify AI investments to her board. Reads content during her morning commute (7:00 AM Bogota). Shares useful posts with her leadership team via WhatsApp. Thinks in quarters and business cases. Follows 3-4 AI voices but trusts the one who understands LATAM corporate reality. She doesn't delegate the actual using of AI — she ships her own automations on weekends and brings working examples to leadership meetings, not just slides.

**Valentina — The Ambitious Professional (ICP-2)**
Senior software engineer at Globant in Medellin, 4 years in. Knows AI is the career differentiator but hasn't figured out how to go from "interesting" to "integrated." Reads content on her phone during breaks. Saves posts she wants to try on the weekend. Shares builder content with colleagues. Today she's learning; in 3 years she's making buying decisions.

**Andrés — The Scaling Founder (ICP-3)**
Co-founder of a 12-person SaaS company in Mexico City. Profitable but resource-constrained. Uses AI daily to do the work of a team twice his size. Reads content during lunch or late at night after his team logs off. Looking for tested workflows, not theory. Will pay for something that saves him 10 hours/week. Shares content with his co-founder via Slack with "we should try this."

### The Primary Bet

Each piece of content targets one ICP at a time. Cadence priority: **Camila first, Valentina second, Andrés third.** Most pieces default to Camila; Valentina pieces are in regular rotation; Andrés pieces are interspersed.

## 2. Knowledge Baseline & Sophistication

**What they already know** (don't explain this):
- AI exists and matters. ChatGPT is a household name. They've used it.
- Companies are adopting AI — they've seen the headlines.
- "Prompting" is a thing. Some have tried Claude, Gemini, Copilot.
- AI agents are the next wave — they've heard the term but most haven't built or deployed one.

**What they think they know but are wrong about**:
- That AI adoption = buying a tool (it's a workflow redesign)
- That AI is too expensive for LATAM companies (costs have collapsed)
- That "being technical" is required to build with AI (builder tools have changed this)
- That they have time to wait and see (the window for first-mover advantage is closing)

**What they don't know yet** (the content opportunity):
- How agentic systems replace entire workflows, not just tasks
- What AI-native companies look like (10x fewer employees per $ revenue)
- Which specific AI tools and architectures are production-ready vs. hype
- How to frame AI investment as business strategy, not IT expense
- What's actually happening at the SF frontier and what it means for their reality

**Sophistication level**: Schwartz Level 3-4 — they're aware of the problem and exploring solutions, but haven't committed to an approach. Don't start from "what is AI?" — start from "here's what to do about it."

**Jargon calibration**:
- Use freely: AI, prompts, agentes, automatización, workflows, Claude Code, n8n, ChatGPT
- Use with brief context: agentic systems, multi-agent, RAG, vector databases, fine-tuning
- Never use without translation: embeddings, tokenization, inference costs, attention mechanisms, architectural patterns

### Domain Focus: Agentic Systems

The content operates in one specific domain: **agentic AI systems** — AI that doesn't just answer questions but takes action, orchestrates workflows, and operates autonomously. This is the highest-leverage capability these tools offer, and it's where the audience needs to go, whether they know it yet or not.

**Where each archetype is on the agentic adoption curve:**

- **Camila (ICP-1)**: Knows "AI agents" is the next conversation her board will have. Has seen demos, maybe a proof of concept internally. Needs to understand what agentic systems mean for her organization's operations, headcount, and competitive position. Needs the business case, not the architecture.
- **Valentina (ICP-2)**: Has heard "AI agents" but it feels advanced and out of reach. Needs to see that building with agentic tools (Claude Code, n8n, Make) is accessible — not just for developers or companies with big budgets. Needs the "I built this and you can too" bridge.
- **Andrés (ICP-3)**: Already using AI tools daily but mostly in single-task mode (chat, copilot, basic automation). Ready to build multi-step workflows and autonomous systems that run parts of his business. Needs tested agentic workflows he can implement — which tools, which patterns, what works in production.

**Content implications:**
- Every topic should connect to how agentic systems change the game — not just "AI tips" but "what happens when AI acts, not just answers"
- The progression is: single-tool use → multi-step automation → autonomous agentic systems. Content meets each archetype where they are and pulls them forward.
- Avoid generic "AI productivity" content that could be about ChatGPT prompts. The bar is higher: how do these tools work as systems, not as chat windows?

## 3. Emotional & Status Drivers

**What makes them feel ahead**:
- Knowing something their peers don't know yet
- Having a framework to explain AI to their team/board/partners
- Being the person in their network who "gets AI"
- Seeing someone build something impressive and thinking "I could do that"

**What makes them feel anxious**:
- Competitors adopting AI while they deliberate
- Reading AI news and not knowing what it means for their company/career
- The feeling that the world is moving faster than they can learn
- Being in a meeting where AI comes up and not having a clear point of view

**The aspiration gap**:
- Where they are: aware, experimenting, uncertain about next steps
- Where they want to be: confident, implementing, ahead of their peers
- The content bridges this gap — not by telling them what to think, but by showing what's possible and giving them the tools to act

**Social currency**: They share content that makes them look informed and forward-thinking. A post they forward to their team or WhatsApp group says "I found this, I'm on top of this." The share IS the status signal.

## 4. Jobs to Be Done (Content-Specific)

### Functional Jobs
- "Help me understand what [trend/tool/announcement] means for my company" (ICP-1, ICP-3)
- "Show me a workflow I can implement this week" (ICP-2, ICP-3)
- "Give me a framework to explain AI adoption to my leadership" (ICP-1)
- "Filter the AI noise — tell me what actually matters" (all tiers)
- "Show me what's real vs. hype so I don't waste budget/time" (ICP-1, ICP-3)

### Emotional Jobs
- "Make me feel like I'm ahead of the curve, not behind it" (all tiers)
- "Give me confidence that I can do this — even if I'm not technical" (ICP-2)
- "Validate that betting on AI is the right move" (ICP-3)
- "Make the complex feel achievable" (all tiers)

### Social Jobs
- "Give me something valuable to share with my team" (ICP-1)
- "Give me something smart to share with my network" (all tiers)
- "Give me something that positions me as forward-thinking" (all tiers)

### Decision Jobs (the specific decisions they face)
- ICP-1: "Should we invest in AI training for our team? Which approach? How do I justify the budget?"
- ICP-1: "Which AI tools should we adopt at the enterprise level?"
- ICP-2: "How do I apply AI to my specific role? What should I learn first?"
- ICP-3: "Which AI tools should I bet my business on? How do I integrate them without breaking what works?"
- ICP-3: "Can I automate X without hiring another person?"

**Frequency**: Daily scan (LinkedIn, 2-3 min per post). Weekly deep-dive (newsletter, 6-8 min). Quarterly strategic input (reports, frameworks).

## 5. Information Diet & Consumption Context

**The moment**: Mobile phone, morning commute or first coffee. Bogota 7-8 AM, Mexico City 8-9 AM, SF equivalent for the 5% US-based. Second window: late evening wind-down (10-11 PM).

**Attention budget**: 2-3 minutes per LinkedIn post (scanning, stops for hooks that promise specific value). 6-8 minutes for newsletter. Screenshots and demos get longer attention than text-only.

**What they already consume**:
- LinkedIn feed (primary discovery channel)
- WhatsApp groups where peers share links and recommendations
- ChatGPT/Claude directly (they experiment themselves)
- Tech news in passing (Bloomberg Linea, TechCrunch headlines, Platzi content)
- Some follow English-language AI voices but most prefer Spanish content that understands their context

**What they've abandoned** (and why):
- Generic AI newsletters that just repackage news (no interpretation, no LATAM context)
- Guru-energy content that promises "10x your revenue" without showing the work
- Overly technical content that assumes developer-level knowledge
- English-only content that requires translation of context, not just language

**Format preference**:
- Short paragraphs (2-3 lines), generous white space, mobile-scannable
- Screenshots and demos over abstract explanations
- Step-by-step when applicable (high save rate)
- Deep dives reserved for newsletter (not LinkedIn)

## 6. Language & Framing Guide

### Words that resonate
- Construir, implementar, automatizar, decisiones, resultados, ventaja, anticipar
- "En X minutos" (specific timeframes = achievable)
- "Lo que significa para tu empresa/equipo/carrera"
- "Lo que nadie te está diciendo sobre..."
- Real numbers ($, hours saved, time to build)

### Words that repel
- Corporate buzzwords: "sinergia", "paradigma", "ecosistema holístico"
- Guru energy: "secreto", "truco infalible", "la clave del éxito"
- Hype without substance: "revolucionario", "game-changer" (unless backed by proof)
- Overly academic: "coadyuvar", "implementación de frameworks de gobernanza"

### Tone calibration
- Formality: 30% formal / 70% conversational — credible but approachable
- Authority: Confident, not arrogant. Shows the work, doesn't lecture.
- Urgency: Present-tense opportunity, not fear-mongering. "This is happening now" > "you'll be left behind"
- Humor: Light, situational, never forced. Okay to be playful when it's natural.
- Tu form always. Never usted. Never guru. Never generic LinkedIn-speak.

### Metaphor domains that land
- Building/construction ("construir", "cimientos", "arquitectura")
- Speed/racing ("ventaja", "adelantarse", "primera movida")
- Exploration/frontier ("lo que se viene", "desde la frontera", "territorio nuevo")

### Cultural context
- Spanglish is natural when tech terms don't have clean translations (AI agent, prompt, workflow)
- LATAM corporate humor: self-aware, slightly irreverent toward bureaucracy
- Directness valued over diplomatic hedging (say what you mean)
- Regional pride matters — content should feel LATAM-native, not translated from English

## 7. Anti-Audience (Who This Is NOT For)

- **Passive consumers** looking for entertainment, not action
- **AI skeptics** who want to argue whether AI matters — that debate is over
- **Tool tourists** hopping between every new AI app without implementing anything
- **Theory-only readers** who want philosophy, not building
- **Anyone expecting usted-form, corporate-safe, diluted content**

**Sophistication floor**: Don't write for someone who hasn't heard of ChatGPT. That reader exists, but optimizing for them would bore the audience that matters.

**Engagement floor**: Optimize for the reader who will DO something after reading — try a tool, share with their team, implement a workflow, make a decision. Not the reader who just "likes" and scrolls.

## 8. Content Decision Filters

When evaluating a topic, angle, or draft, run these checks:

### Topic filter (should I cover this?)
1. Would Camila (ICP-1) bring this up in a leadership meeting?
2. Would Valentina (ICP-2) save it to try on the weekend?
3. Does this connect to a decision the audience is actively making?
4. Is there a LATAM-specific angle that English-language coverage misses?

If fewer than 2 are yes → kill the topic.

### Angle filter (how should I frame this?)
1. What does the audience already know about this? (Don't repeat it — build on it)
2. What would surprise them? (Lead with the surprising part)
3. What action can they take after reading? (If none, reconsider)
4. Does this frame make Camila want to forward it? Does it make Valentina want to save it?

### Depth filter (how much detail?)
- LinkedIn: insight + proof + one action. 2-3 min read max.
- Newsletter: full framework + step-by-step + real examples. 6-8 min.
- If you can't say it in a LinkedIn post, it's a newsletter topic.
- If it needs code or architecture details, it's too deep for any format.

### Tone check
- Does this sound like something said over coffee with a sharp colleague? → Ship it
- Does this sound like a corporate presentation or a motivational poster? → Rewrite or kill it

## 10. What Performs (March 2026 — Validated)

**Top post**: "No te imaginas mi cara cuando vi $5K en mi cuenta" — 74,521 impressions, 511 engagements
- Builder mode. Real outcome. Emotional hook. Personal story with specific number.

**Second**: "En SF muchos compraron Mac Minis" — 21,386 impressions, 596 engagements
- Frontier signal + practical insight. "What's happening at the frontier and what it means."

**Third**: "Ayer recibo un mensaje: Irina, ganamos" — 11,203 impressions, 213 engagements
- Achievement story. Builder proof with a specific win.

**Pattern across all top 10**: Builder mode + personal story + specific outcome. Zero philosopher-mode posts performed.

### Engagement triggers (validated)
| Trigger | Why it works for this audience |
|---|---|
| Specific outcome ($5K, won hackathon, built in 30 min) | Proof over promise — they trust evidence |
| Frontier signal ("In SF I saw...") | Information they can't get from their current sources |
| Time constraint ("in X minutes") | Makes it feel achievable for their busy schedule |
| Resistance-to-breakthrough ("I was resistant until...") | Validates their own hesitation, then shows the path |
| Visible result (screenshot, demo) | Undeniable proof they can evaluate themselves |
| Share CTA ("Repostealo para tu red...") | Activates their desire to be the person who shares valuable intel |

### Content that fails (validated)
- English posts — catastrophic performance, no exceptions
- Philosopher mode — abstract concepts without building/doing anchor
- Borrowed authority — quotes without personal experience
- Encyclopedia lists — "7 AI Concepts" without grounding in action
- Generic motivation — could be posted by anyone
- Architecture diagrams — too technical even for ICP-1
- Promo-first posts — selling before value
