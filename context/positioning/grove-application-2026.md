## Application Answers (As Submitted)

### Question 1: What are you building?

Across LATAM enterprises, knowledge workers particularly in IT and telecom are being asked to adopt AI in their daily work. While AI resources and training exist, many employees still lack practical, implementation-oriented guidance that is accessible, and available in Spanish.

Based on my experience, the challenge is not awareness of AI, but effective enablement: translating abstract capabilities into concrete workflows that fit existing roles, constraints, and organizational realities.

I am building the foundations of an AI enablement ecosystem designed to address this gap. The focus is on helping knowledge workers in medium and large enterprises use AI effectively, starting with IT and telecom employees who are under strong pressure to adopt AI.

My approach uses content-led distribution as the core moat. I'm building the audience first through LinkedIn/TikTok, treating these platforms as continuous customer discovery.

This distribution engine serves dual purpose: validate product ideas before building and create direct channel to my target audience when products launch.

I'm reducing the practical barrier to AI adoption. I bridge the gap between high-quality AI resources that are predominantly available in English and the limited availability of implementation-focused material in Spanish, using my own learning journey and hands-on experimentation as the bridge.

The business strategy follows a bottom-up approach by creating awareness and demand for AI capability-building at the employee level inside medium and large enterprises with established IT departments.

I am uniquely positioned to build this because I spent 10 years working in corporate LATAM IT/telecom infrastructure, so I understand the mental models, organizational constraints, and implementation friction involved in technology adoption because I experienced them firsthand.

---

### Question 2: What have you built previously?

My learning journey is my main product. I came from corporate telecom data center management and have been documenting my transition into becoming an AI builder. The learning path I have followed allows me to guide my audience by acting as a bridge between technical and non-technical perspectives.

While this is not yet a final product, I have been shipping public artifacts across three platforms: Notion (structured study notes), GitHub (live vibe-coding experiments), and LinkedIn as the public repository of my journey. These artifacts are designed to translate AI concepts into practical insights that knowledge workers can apply within their existing roles.

This includes a recent project in which I built a content engine using a multi-agent architecture based on Markdown files to accelerate my content creation workflow.

As a result of this ongoing public work, I was selected by Favikon as a Top 10 female creator in Colombia in the Cloud Computing and Data Center category in November 2025.

---

### Question 3: What do you hope to gain from OpenAI Grove?

One of the most challenging aspects for me has been learning how to navigate a world where I have never been. I realized that the first thing I needed to work on was rewiring my mindset for entrepreneurship, because the mental models I developed in the corporate world do not fully apply to this entrepreneurial path I choose.

I see Grove as a unique environment to compress first-time founder learning from years into months. By being surrounded by other builders and mentors, I expect to accelerate how I adopt effective builder patterns, add pressure to my assumptions, and validate both the market and business logic while I'm still shaping what I'm creating.

In 2025, my biggest setback was trying to build alone and from home. That experience reinforced the idea that environment compounds learning as much as curiosity and personal agency. That insight, changed how I was approaching the building process:

I decided to come back to San Francisco after attending Andrew Ng's first AI Dev 25 event last March. I am currently here to actively engage with the builder community, attend events, and immerse myself in an environment that accelerates my learning and execution.

---

### Closing: Why you + Why now

I spent 10 years translating cloud technologies for a telecom company in LATAM , acting as the bridge between large technology providers (Google, AWS, Oracle, Microsoft…) and the realities of traditional, non–digital-native organizations. Today, I am applying that same bridging role to AI.

My unfair advantage is communication rather than coding, combined with the curiosity and discipline to learn how to vibe-code and learn what is needed to build with AI effectively. My background is a key differentiator because it gives me a deep understanding of Colombian and Mexican corporate mental models and their constraints from the perspective of non–digital-native organizations.

I know how to translate new technology capabilities from an adoption perspective, what we often call "tropicalizing" in LATAM, by turning comprehensive frameworks into problem-focused solutions aligned with organizations' capabilities and maturity levels.
