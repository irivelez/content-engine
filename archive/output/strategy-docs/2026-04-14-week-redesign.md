## THE CORE DIAGNOSIS (Corrected)

### What Actually Went Wrong with 4/14

The 4/14 post did **806 impressions, 17 reactions, 0 comments, 2 profile views, 2 followers, 2 link clicks**.

The real problem wasn't video absence or format. It was **positioning**:

**The post was unpaid Anthropic marketing.** It opened with "Anthropic lanzo 20+ features" (their subject). It listed their features. It used their product names. It ended with "follow me for more Anthropic features."

Irina was the messenger, not the authority. The audience correctly read this as: *"this person is sharing product news."* Product news gets saves (9), maybe reactions (17), and zero profile curiosity (2) because there's nothing about the messenger to be curious about.

Compare to Irina's best posts:
- **"ayer recibo un mensaje: Irina ganamos"** → Irina is the subject. The hackathon is the frame.
- **"La mayoría instala Claude Code y lo usa como un chatbot caro. Yo lo convertí en un equipo de desarrollo"** → Irina vs the majority. Claude Code is the proof.
- **"La IA no te simplificó la vida. Te subió el estándar 20x"** → Irina's observation. Karpathy is the reference.

**The pattern**: Irina's best posts START with Irina's decision/observation/outcome. Features are evidence, never the subject.

### Why "No Video" Is Not the Problem

The 4/14 post doesn't need video. It needs **Irina's lens**. The entire post could have been rewritten without a minute of video time and performed 5-10x better by starting with:

> "Cancelé 3 suscripciones este mes. Claude absorbió todas sus funciones. Aquí las 20+ features que ya reemplazan tu stack actual..."

Same content. Same infographic. But now Irina made a decision (canceled subscriptions), made a claim (Claude absorbed functions), and gives the reader strategic ammunition, not feature PR.

## THE META-INSIGHT

The difference between Irina working for Anthropic vs Irina working for Irina is ONE SENTENCE — the first sentence of each post.

If the first sentence names Anthropic's product, Irina is the messenger.
If the first sentence names Irina's decision, Irina is the authority.

Everything downstream follows this choice. The same content, with the hook moved, becomes either PR copy or thought leadership.

**This is the most important craft discipline for executive-targeted content**: always start with YOUR stake, not their product.

The rest of the week's system — lead magnets, CTAs, visuals — just compounds the positioning you chose with that first sentence.
