## PART 1: WHY 4/14 UNDERPERFORMED (Post-mortem)

### The 4/14 Post
- **Hook**: "Anthropic lanzo 20+ features de Claude en 4 meses"
- **Format**: Exhaustive list of 20+ features grouped by product
- **Visual**: Dense timeline infographic (beautiful but visually overwhelming)
- **CTA**: "sigueme para que no te lo pierdas"

### The 5 Things That Killed Performance

**1. Announcement mode, not builder mode**
The hook is third-person news: "Anthropic lanzo..." Compare to your best performers:
- "$5K en mi cuenta" → Personal stake (YOU)
- "En SF muchos compraron Mac Minis" → Frontier observation (YOU in a specific place)
- "Llevo meses usando Claude Code. Pero hasta hace 3 semanas descubri..." → Personal discovery (YOU)

The 4/14 hook has no Irina. Your audience doesn't follow you for Anthropic PR — they follow you for what YOU build and see.

**2. Overwhelm over curiosity**
20+ features in one post = cognitive overload. The LinkedIn algorithm measures dwell time AND action. People see the wall of features, save for later, never return.

Compare: Allie's best posts focus on ONE specific thing deeply (the 64-year-old NYC woman, the $80 Atlas shopping experiment). Depth > breadth for engagement.

**3. No funnel layer**
"Sigueme" is a weak CTA. No email list growth. No PDF download. No Sprint AI signup. The post generated zero measurable business value even if engagement had been good.

This is THE critical gap in your current content: every post should feed ONE of: (a) email list, (b) PDF downloads, (c) Sprint AI signups.

**4. The infographic competed with the post**
Dense visuals work when they reinforce ONE idea. Your timeline had so many features it became a reference document, not a social proof point. People save it instead of engaging with it.

**5. No tension in the hook**
"Anthropic lanzo 20+ features" is a fact. Facts don't stop scrolls. Tension stops scrolls:
- "Ningun lab esta publicando a esta velocidad. Y el 90% solo conoce el 10%." ← This would have been stronger as the hook

### The Lesson
**The 4/14 post was a newsletter article disguised as a LinkedIn post.** It belongs in the Beehiiv newsletter (where long-form works). On LinkedIn, you compress. On the newsletter, you expand.

## PART 2: THE MACHINE LAYER (What This Week Needs)

Each of the 6 drafts is well-written at the content level. What's missing is **the machine layer** — the systematic funnel integration that turns each post from a content piece into a business engine.

### The 4-Layer Stack

Every post this week (and from now on) should have:

```
LAYER 1: VOICE-ALIGNED HOOK (you do this well)
   ↓
LAYER 2: BUILDER-PROOF BODY (you do this well)
   ↓
LAYER 3: FUNNEL BRIDGE (MISSING from current drafts)
   ↓
LAYER 4: SPECIFIC CTA (MISSING — currently all rhetorical questions)
```

### Funnel Bridge Options (Per Post)

| Bridge Type | When to Use | Example |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| **Direct PDF link** | When the post topic maps to an existing PDF | "Descarga el stack completo que uso: [link]" |
| **Sprint AI plug** | When the post topic is a Sprint AI lesson | "Esto es exactamente lo que hacemos en el Dia 2 de Sprint AI: [link]" |
| **Lara-style trigger** | When you want to capture emails AND boost reposts | "Repostea esto y te mando la plantilla completa en DM" |
| **Bio link trust** | When the body already built massive authority | "Todos los recursos estan en mi perfil" (passive, Allie-style) |

At 3,400 followers, you need the **explicit CTAs** (options 1-3), not the **passive CTA** (option 4). Allie's passive approach works at 1.5M. For now, copy Lara's tactical aggression.
